Reuse Poster

One of the assignments in my 2D Fundamentals class was to design a persuasive poster. I chose to design a poster to encourage people to use reusable shopping bags instead of plastic ones. These are just a few of the many versions I came up with, and reading from left to right and top to bottom gives an idea of the process I went through in designing this. The bottom right hand design is the final version.

Path To Design Poster

In my Design: Process, Context, Systems class, I was tasked with interviewing a classmate and coming up with a visual expression of her path to design. I decided to design a series of movie posters where each one represented a person, experience, or event that led her to study design. I designed each movie poster separately and then had to figure out the best way to put them all together. Again, I'm showing several versions I went through as part of my design process.

Prototypes

MBTA Community Bulletin Board

This prototype of a mobile app was made in Figma as a design project for my Design Process Context/Systems class. I wanted to address the lack of community building and engagement while people waited for public transportation, so I decided to create a mobile app that would work in tandem with digital bulletin boards. The digital bulletin boards would be located inside MBTA stations, and the idea is that it would act as a forum for community members to have conversations with each other and learn about local happenings. Users would use the mobile app to post content to the boards, and the app also has a chat function to help members of the community connect with each other.

Oasis Website

This is a prototype of a website for an online subscription box company called Oasis (disclaimer: not a real company). The final project for my Design Process Context/Systems class was to design something of use during the COVID19 pandemic. My group decided to tackle the safety risk in physically going outside to buy goods by creating a subscription box service so people can get food, workout equipment, basic toiletries, and more delivered to their homes.

The checkout possibilities for this prototype were too many, so this prototype only makes sense when buying workout equipment first, then buying pantry staples, then checking out.
